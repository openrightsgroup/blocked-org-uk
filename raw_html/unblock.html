How to get a site unblocked

Unfortunately there are no standards, industry codes of conduct, or centralised tools to address overblocking. If you think a site is being censored inappropriately by an ISP you must contact them individually and hope they take corrective action.

On this page we've tried to detail how to report problems to the networks we monitor. Where we don't have complete information we've listed general contact details instead.

If you can help us improve the information on this page or expand it to cover other networks, or if you've tried to get a site unblocked and either succeeded or failed, please *****contact us***** and share what you know.

Home broadband networks

Andrews & Arnold

Our sponsors Andrews & Arnold only provide unfiltered internet connections so you should never need to contact them about a blocked page. If you're having trouble accessing a site, though, their technical support page explains how to get in touch with them.

BT

We couldn't find any information on BT's website about how to correct faulty filtering. They do have a complaints page though.

Plusnet

We asked Jess how to report that Plusnet's Parental Controls are blocking a site incorrectly but she didn't seem to know. They do have a contact page however it isn't obvious how a non-customer can make a complaint.

Other

If you've come across censorship on another home broadband network, please *****let us know (contact us)*****.

Sky

Sites blocked incorrectly by Sky Broadband Shield can be reported to Sky by email. Their troubleshooting guide has more information.

TalkTalk

TalkTalk say they will add websites to blocked categories if a customer asks them to - and if their "panel of online experts" agree. They don't say whether this works the other way round too. Nor could we find any information on how a non-customer, such as a website owner, can complain about a wrongly-blocked site. Here's TalkTalk's contact page.

Virgin Media

Sites blocked incorrectly by Virgin Media's Child Safe product can be reported to them by email. There's more information in this thread on their community forum.

Mobile networks

EE

Everything Everywhere have set up an email address to receive reports of misclassification by their Content Lock product. This address can also be used to report problems with Orange Safeguard and T-Mobile Content Lock. More information is available on their website.

O2

O2 used to have a URL checker but they shut it down some time ago. They suggest letting them know about filtering errors via their Twitter account, @O2, or through their forum instead. There's more information on their blog.

Other

If you've experienced overblocking on another mobile network, please *****tell us about it (contact us)*****.

Three

Three say, "Sometimes a website can be blocked in error. If you think there’s been a mistake, or you come across a website that’s not blocked when it should be, then email us at content@three.co.uk and we'll acknowledge it within 24 hours."

Vodafone

For the moment, Vodafone have asked that these requests go to this email address. They are planning to have a more specific address available soon. They also have a complaints page.

Help find a permanent solution

Getting a site unblocked is only a temporary fix, it could get blocked again and more sites will be incorrectly classified in the future. The only way to prevent sites from being blocked is to stop the censorship.
